About

Regulatory Affairs & Quality Assurance Professional | MSc Organic Chemistry

I am a detail-oriented Regulatory Affairs and Quality Assurance professional with a strong scientific foundation and hands-on academic project experience in Health Canada regulatory frameworks, Natural Health Products (NHPs), pharmacovigilance, and compliance documentation. With a Master’s degree in Organic Chemistry and formal training in Regulatory Affairs, I bring a structured, evidence-based approach to ensuring product quality, patient safety, and regulatory compliance across the product lifecycle.

My background bridges science, regulation, and quality systems. I have worked on regulatory submission simulations, label compliance reviews, adverse event case assessments, and SOP-driven quality processes, gaining practical exposure to how regulatory expectations are applied in real-world industry settings. I am particularly interested in desk-based roles involving regulatory research, dossier preparation, post-market surveillance, and quality documentation.

I am skilled in interpreting regulatory guidance and translating complex requirements into clear, compliant documentation. Through academic and independent projects, I have developed experience in Health Canada (NNHPD), GMP principles, CAPA concepts, pharmacovigilance reporting, and regulatory gap analysis. I am also familiar with the responsible use of AI tools (ChatGPT, NotebookLM) to support regulatory research and drafting while maintaining compliance, traceability, and data integrity.

I am actively seeking entry-level or junior roles in Regulatory Affairs, Quality Assurance, or Pharmacovigilance within the Canadian life sciences, pharmaceutical, medical device, or natural health product industries, where I can contribute to compliance-driven teams and continue developing as a regulatory professional.

Health Canada Risk Management Plan (RMP)

Group presentation analyzing regulatory requirements for drug safety risk management.

Project Overview

Developed a comprehensive group presentation on Health Canada’s Risk Management Plan (RMP) framework as part of the REGA 5023 course at Humber College. The project focused on understanding RMPs as a proactive drug safety tool within the post-market lifecycle, aligned with Canadian and ICH regulatory expectations.

Key Highlights
  • Explained the purpose, scope, and regulatory importance of RMPs in Canada.
  • Reviewed when RMPs are required for drug submissions and post-market updates.
  • Analyzed standard RMP sections including safety specification, pharmacovigilance plans, and risk minimization measures.
  • Highlighted Canadian-specific addendum requirements and Health Canada review processes.
  • Collaborated in a team setting to deliver a clear, structured regulatory presentation.

Food and Drug Regulations (FDR) Flowchart Analysis

Group-based regulatory mapping of Health Canada’s Food and Drug Regulations.

Project Overview

Developed a detailed regulatory flowchart outlining the structure and application of Health Canada’s Food and Drug Regulations (FDR) as part of the REGA 5022 course at Humber College. The project focused on interpreting key regulatory Parts, Divisions, and requirements governing drug approval, manufacturing, labelling, and clinical trials in Canada.

Key Highlights
  • Analyzed major Parts of the Food and Drug Regulations, including Parts A, C, G, and J.
  • Mapped Division-specific requirements for drug approval, sale, labelling, and distribution.
  • Reviewed Division 8 requirements related to new drug submissions and clinical trial authorization.
  • Summarized regulatory controls for biologics, controlled substances, and restricted drugs.
  • Collaborated with team members to design a clear, visual regulatory flowchart and present findings.
